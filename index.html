The Ca' Granda (Milano)

A guide for visiting the Cà Granda in the center of Milan. Built in the Fifteenth century, the former hospital is now a campus for the University of Milan.

The Ca' Granda (La Ca' Granda)

The Ice-House Courtyard

The Courtyard

At the center of the courtyard, a glass-covered hole in the ground is all that remains of the titular ice-house, its above-ground structure destroyed during World War 2. The structure is accessible through the underground Philosophy library and was managed by the hospital staff.

The first mention of the ice-house dates back to November 1638. Referred to as a cella nivaria, the structure was created to pile away snow during wintertime, which was then pressed and used to store food and as treatment for the patients of the hospital.

The Ice-House

Tucked away in a corner of the Ca' Granda, the ice-house is one of the most recognizable parts of the complex, though it wasn't part of the original design. Originally a cemetery built in 1473 by master mason Antonio da Rosate, it was moved to a different location, along with its chapel, and replaced by the apothecary's workshop before the building was turned into the dispensary.
